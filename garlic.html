Garlic

Medicinal Uses

Garlic has been used for thousands of years for both culinary and medicinal purposes. Its medicinal uses include:

  • Boosting the immune system
  • Reducing blood pressure and cholesterol levels
  • Possessing antimicrobial and antiviral properties
  • Aiding in digestion and reducing gastrointestinal issues
  • Potentially improving cardiovascular health

Scientific Name

The scientific name of garlic is Allium sativum. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family.

Habitat

Garlic is native to Central Asia and has been cultivated globally. It thrives in well-drained soil with a sunny location. Garlic is commonly grown in temperate regions and can be cultivated in a variety of climates, making it a popular choice for gardens around the world.
