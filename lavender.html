Lavender

Medicinal Uses

Lavender is widely known for its calming and therapeutic properties. Its medicinal uses include:

  • Reducing anxiety and stress
  • Improving sleep quality and treating insomnia
  • Relieving headaches and migraines
  • Alleviating symptoms of depression
  • Soothing minor burns and insect bites

Scientific Name

The scientific name of lavender is Lavandula angustifolia (also known as Lavandula officinalis).

Habitat

Lavender is native to the Mediterranean region but is now cultivated worldwide. It thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight. Lavender is commonly grown in gardens and farms in temperate climates, where it can benefit from warm, sunny conditions and minimal humidity.
